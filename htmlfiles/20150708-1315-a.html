The situation with Euphonium is somewhat unique for Kyoani.

Euphonium is neither a novel that was written for the sole purpose of being adapted by Kyoani (like the source material for Free, Chuunibyou, etc.), nor was a novel owned by a publishing company that contacted Kyoani and hired them to make it into an anime adaptation (like Haruhi, Hyouka, etc.).

Euphonium was originally a novel published by a completely unrelated publishing company, written by an author with no connection to Kyoani.
A few Kyoani employees read the novels, and liked them enough to convince the rest of the company to try to make an anime adaptation of it. So they contacted the publisher and eventually bought the rights to the anime adaptation. They are the sole owner of the right for the anime adaptation and can do whatever they want with it.

I'm generalizing a tad, since opportunity cost in general really depends on the person's own idea of what's worth their time or not. But in general learning antoher language could be considered an opportunity cost simply because of the large amount of time you have to spend on it.

In this case context is extremely important, if you were to say be learning another language so that you can be more efficient in your job that means interacting with people on an international level. Then it could very well be considered an investment of sorts, and thus the opportunity cost isn't all that much since you are doing so in a way that will make up for that lost time in your workplace.
