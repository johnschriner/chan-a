燃え尽きそうな空に歌は響き、 明日を輝かす種火となる。
"There is a song that stirs in the sky that's burnt out. It will be an ember that brightens tomorrow."
That's literal, but reads stiff. That's bad.

"A resounding ballad shakes the charred sky above. From within, a spark is born that shall shine onto the future."
Sounds prettier, but it's too far off the original and uses needless word substitution and lets the translators voice bleed in too much. "Amidst the scorched skies a song cries out. It becomes the ember that illuminates tomorrow."
This uses the original verbiage, keeps things in the right order, doesn't add any new or unnecessary ideas, and is still written in roughly equally "poetic" prose. This is the medium people should aim for. It keeps everything in tact and won't present any problems down the line if any of these ideas should return later on to play an important role.
