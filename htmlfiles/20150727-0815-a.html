Qliphoth is derived from Kabbalah, which is indeed Jewish mysticism, but Berserk's usage of the term is entirely different.

In Kabbalah's cosmology, the world is essentially an "emanation" of an absolute fundamental principle, "God." God in Kabbalah is not an anthropomorphic being, but an underlying eternal concept from which the whole works of creation sprung that is beyond individual human comprehension, very similar to Hinduism's Brahman or, in a more tangible capacity, the Dao (in Daoism). Essentially, "God" wished to "know itself" and to do so split its perception into multiple different perspectives, eventually coming to humanity.

There's quite a few aspects dealing with Platonic ideals and how reality is derived from them, the most prominent being Adam Kadmon, the archetype of humanity, as well as the Sephiroth, which is more complicated. But basically what you need to understand is that everyone is an derived from/emanation of God in Kabbalah and therefore exists to better understand God through themselves and others. Qliphoth, which means "husk," is a corrupting force which represents the external/material forces of the world preventing you from attaining internal understanding, thus cockblocking your role in the goals of God, who you are (along with everyone else).

That's the cliff-notes version. So you can kinda see where Miura got the inspiration, but it's pretty different from how it's generally treated in the actual cosmology.
