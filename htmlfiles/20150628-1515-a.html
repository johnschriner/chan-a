Actually, to be more precise, There is no correct translation of that term in English.
It's a Japanese tradition that doesn't even exist in the west.
There are proper acknowledgements to having a meal. Like a greeting.
The served will say "Itadakimasu" = basically thank you for the meal, I will have them now.
Once they are finished eating, they say "Gochisou sama" = basically I have finished the meal, thank you
To which the server replies "Osomatsu sama" = You're welcome but no big deal, it wasn't much but it's the least I could do.
The closest equivalent would be "you're welcome"

As pointed out before, お粗末 (osomatsu) has the traditional Japanese humility and "it wasn't much" is as close as we get in English - in fact, it's a literal translation that happens to sound fine. "Happy to serve" doesn't sound as modest and the Japanese equivalent would be closer to 毎度あり.
