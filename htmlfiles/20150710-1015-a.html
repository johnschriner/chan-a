Carbonara sauce is made out of beaten eggs, grated pecorino romano cheese and black pepper.

Mixing it creates a creamy sauce (more so if you put in more yolks over the whites) and pouring it over newly cooked pasta and sauted Italian bacon (guanciale) creates a very rich, flavorful and delicious dish. The heat from the noodles and bacon will cook the egg but not enough to turn it to scrambled. Throw in some chopped parsley for a kick.

Tossing cream is a big no-no since not only will the cream take away the taste, it pretty much takes away the flavor, richness, texture and the like that gives it its identity as carbonara. That and why pour cream when you already have the egg/cheese batter cream set on it?

I know, I know, cooking is a very open craft. Experiment is key in this field but at the same time, there's a line in dishes where you just stop. Carbonara doesn't need cream, not even mushrooms or peas and onions for the same reason you don't put ketchup on steak. It's hard to describe it (elitism? autism? I dunno) but that's just how it is.
