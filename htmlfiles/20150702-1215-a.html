Agreed. Generally when you look at an object in a photograph you aren't trying to replicate it perfectly anyways. Hell if you want to do that you're already done just keep the photograph. The idea is to abstract the essence of the object, that's how we can get still, non moving objects at the apex of a motion to give off the illusion of movement. A lot of factors affect this such as line weight, line cadence, weight displacement, muscle definition, lighting, etc. One anon said it earlier that you really have to understand the object you are looking at, and that's the only way to do it. There's no magic "quick fix".
