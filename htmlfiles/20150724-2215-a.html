1. Learning radicals, useful for looking up in electronic dictionaries and distinguishing between similar looking Kanji when you need to.
2. Learning stroke order, useful for looking up in electronic dictionaries and deciphering handwriting and weird fonts.
3. Learning a unique meaning for a Kanji, useful when learning vocabulary because the Kanji it's made from usually hint to the actual meaning of the word. I know it's the other way around, vocabulary are what give meaning to the Kanji but you can use this relationship to your advantage.

6. Mnemonics are useful for language learning or memorization in general. It's a good technique to learn.
