Polybius states that the problem with the Roman cavalry lance is that it does not possess a spike on the end of the shaft and that, if the head breaks, the weapon becomes useless. Hanson interprets this passage as referring to all Roman spears in general; not just the lance of the cavalry. From this he extrapolates that the Roman infantry spear was not as good as that of the Greek hoplite due to its lack of a sauroter.
