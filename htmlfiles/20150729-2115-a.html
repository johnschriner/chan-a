Still waiting for Season 3, but lets talk about why this series was so appealing in the first place.

Gateway anime of the worst kind.
I think Haruhi appealed to me in the first place, because of two central themes The first being, "The extraordinary becoming the ordinary."

The Haruhi series is extraordinary, with its aliens, time travellers and espers. But Haruhi doesn't know about their existence. And in Haruhi's perspective, the everyday is the same, and Life is a futile chase after something that is 'not there'. In contrast to Kyon's POV, he sees the things that Haruhi had created but, and as extraordinary as it was, it has become normal to him, partly because said agents want to keep the status quo, and partly because he wnats to get too involved.

I think that, for me, is why Haruhi stands out. A series that tries to blend fantasy with slice of life either have the fantasy as something that doesn't gel well with the ongoing circumstance (Evangelion comes to mind), or the fantasy is one big cover up while normal lives are ongoing (like Shakugan no Shana and Fate/Stay night). But In Haruhi, especially from Kyon's perspective, the fantastic is actually out in the open - its just that its creator cannot visibly see it.

It reminds me of NieA_7 for some odd reason. In that anime, an alien landing had become so accepted, life became mundane. In Haruhi's world, time travel, aliens and espers are possible but, and because its so well hidden, Haruhi is not aware of it.








The other themes, thats central to both Haruhi & NieA_7, is the point of existence, and the meaning of life.

Although we don't know what Haruhi did, exactly, that gave her the powers that she had, we know ''why' she developed them. It was because of the supposed futility and meaninglessness of the individual existence. Its also why she embarks on her seemingly pointless quest: because she wanst to experience the extraordinary.

Its not a new theme: NieA did explore that. But Haruhi does it better because it actually shows how one can make a difference in their lives, by doing something so recognizable that people would not easily forget it (rememebr Live Alive?) NieA only glances at it, Haruhi actually explores it.

This comes full circle in the movie: Kyon' resented the life that he ahd lived within the SOS Brigade. But, when a normal life without it was offered to him, Kyon rejected it. Why?

Because, what point is there in life if everything was so simple? Life was definitely more interesting and exciting when the conflict was there. Its like how Mayuko came to value NieA a lot more after she disappeared - the parallel was the same for Kyon when he lost Haruhi in his life.









A lot of series start out with a great, interesting premise, then have horrible development because the author never planned anything to get revealed.

Haruhi spreads out the exposition of its premise over its entire run, so the slice-of-life bullshit looks like it's hinting at something the viewer doesn't know.
