Anime is produced in various resolutions nowadays, ranging from 720p to 1080p.

However, Japanese HD broadcasts, like HD broadcasts everywhere, are in 1080i by default, with 720p alternatives.

720p > 1080i for anything that has a lot of fast motion or complex action. 1080i loses half its frame rate when converted directly to 1080p, and applying a deinterlacing filter on it can make it difficult to run on a lot of media players. That's why fansubs use 720p encodes, which overall have higher quality for action sequences and scale to 1080p without losing fps.

Or rather, that was the case, since things have changed. Higher native resolutions + abysmal downscaling by sites like Funi have made 720p downscales noticeably worse than 1080p upscales.
